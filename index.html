Is privacy dead?

It is estimated that by 2026, there's a 1/7 chance that modern day encryption will be cracked. Cryptographers and researchers alike are studying new attempts to secure communication post Quantum era.

A real reason of concern

Google, IBM, and other governments are racing to build Quantum Computers. These computers will make WhatsApp, Signal, Gmail, and all communication 100% insecure.

Repeating the same mistakes

Decrypting a message will end up with 1 of 3 things:

1: The correct message, like "Password Correct!"
2: An illegible message, like "03hf8wr9uib"
3: An error message, like "Incorrect Password!"

How do you crack it?
Simple, try every possible password until you get a correct message. This will only get easier from Quantum Computers. Not even RSA can survive.

Why Sliicy?

One word, MEANING. Sliicy messes with the meaning of the message, and words become parts of speech. No Artificial Intelligence or human being will EVER be able to tell what you originally meant.

It is impossible to know if you meant wet or dry, white or black, Smith or Michael, in or out, etc.

More ways Sliicy protects you:

Passwords

Sliicy requires a password that changes all the words every time it's used.

Code

Open source and tested. New words are added frequently. Only trust Sliicy.com.

Serverless

Decentralized, with no single point of failure and no use of servers or Peer-to-Peer networks.

Free

Because why shouldn't it be? Sliicy is free for personal use only. Commercial negotiations are available.

Compatible

Sliicy works on any other app you want. WhatsApp, Gmail, SMS; you can even call up your friend and say it over the phone! No internet needed!

Bulletproof

Sliicy encrypts messages based on the MEANING, not by any fancy math formulas.

Expandable

Sliicy can be translated into other languages too! See GitHub for details.

Words and Numbers

Double words require a dash (-) like "New-York" to be recognized as a single word.

New Words

Words never used before are bit-wise encrypted (letter by letter).

Signing

Sliicy prevents exploiters from MITM (Man-in-the-middle) attacks with a number as the signature.

Data

Support for images, audio and video is also currently being worked on.

Messages

Messages are copy-pasted by the user into and out of the app. Sliicy logs nothing.

How it works:


Every word is a part of speech, such as nouns, adjectives, verbs, adverbs, conjunctions, etc...
Each word in the message is replaced with another word that is still grammatically accurate. The new chosen words are randomized in this demo, but remain consistent when setting up Sliicy. There are over 500 numbers which each affect the message in a different way.

The outcome of each encryption depends on the amount of words, the amount of punctuation, the type of word, and the position of the word in the message.
